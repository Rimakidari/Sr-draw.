Drawing Tips

Let's learn some tips together

Have the right pencil

Having the right pencil is important. The hardness of the graphite is indicated on the side of the pencil: 'B' pencils are softer, 'H' are harder, and 'HB' sits in the middle – there's a big difference between a 4H and a 4B."I recommend starting somewhere on the H scale as a foundation and then finishing with the darker B scale," says travelling convention artist Tim Von Rueden.

Hold your pencil correctly

"If you position your hand closer to the end of the pencil, you have more control and precision, but heavier strokes (darker markings)," says illustrator Sylwia Bomba.

Minimise the amount your hand smudges your pencil lines

"When shading, use an extra piece of paper underneath your hand," advises artist Brun Croes. "This will minimise the amount your hand smudges your pencil lines. If you're right-handed, start shading from left to right; if you're left-handed, start at the right and move to the left.

Create smooth

"Sometimes it's preferable for your shading to be less sketchy and more smooth and subtle," says artist Marisa Lewis.

Avoid symmetrical drawings

"I like symmetrical drawings, but they often look boring all too quickly," says Croes.

Create realistic sketches first

"Once you understand how to recreate something realistically, it becomes incredibly easy to then create a stylised version of the same object, especially for creatures and characters" Von Rueden

Create contrast

"Heavy contrast will demand attention," emphasises Von Rueden. "I recommend placing the highest value contrast in your area of intent focus. You can also add contrast to separate forms and distinguish different subject matter."

Avoid creating contrast everywhere

the softer, low-value contrast piece enables the eye to wander and doesn’t define an area of focus" says Von Rueden.

Use abstract backgrounds

"The use of irregular lines when shading adds a lot of dynamism to your sketch," Bomba says.

Add character to your sketch

"Instead of just looking at what makes an object recognisable you should also consider how to make this object distinctive" says Von Rueden.
